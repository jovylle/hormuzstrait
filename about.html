About this dashboard

This is an independent, educational dashboard focused on how much oil and cargo is flowing through the Strait of Hormuz.

Live ship traffic numbers are sourced from hormuzstraitmonitor.com via their public dashboard API. That project itself aggregates data from services such as MarineTraffic, VesselFinder, oil market feeds, and shipping insurance data.

This site does not represent any government, company, or institution. The goal is to give ordinary people — especially overseas workers and seafarers — a clear visual sense of whether traffic is:

  • close to normal levels,
  • slow and reduced, or
  • showing signs of serious disruption.

For Filipino users, the idea is simple: if ships and tankers keep moving through Hormuz, global trade and oil flows are closer to normal. If the lines stay very low for many days, that can signal higher risk for jobs, fuel prices, and shipping routes.
